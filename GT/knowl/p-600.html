Assume that \(X \) and \(Y \) are locally compact hausdorff, and that \(f \colon X \to Y \) is continuous. Show that the following are equivalent.

  1. The map \(f\) is proper.

  2. For any topological space \(Z\text{,}\) the map \(\id\times f \colon Z \times X \to Z \times Y \) is closed.

  3. The map \(f\) is closed, and for every point \(y\in Y\text{,}\) the preimage \(f^{-1}\{y\}\) is compact.
