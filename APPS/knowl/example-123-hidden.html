To find the matrix representation of the cycle \(f=\big(2\ 5\ 4\ 7\big)\text{,}\) observe that for \(k\not\in \{2,5,4,7\}\text{,}\) \(f(k)=k\text{.}\) Moreover, the cycle notation tells us that \(f(2)=5\text{,}\) \(f(5)=4\text{,}\) \(f(4)=7\) and \(f(7)=2\text{.}\) Thus, \(f\) has matrix representation \(f=\left(\begin{matrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \amp 5 \amp 6 \amp 7 \cr1 \amp 5 \amp 3 \amp 7 \amp 4 \amp 6 \amp 2 \cr \end{matrix} \right)\in\mathrm{S}_{7}\text{.}\)
