Find all integer solutions to \(2x=5y\text{.}\)

Suppose \((x,y)\) are integers solving \(2x=5y\text{.}\) Then CorollaryÂ 8.3.4 implies \(2\) divides \(y\) and \(5\) divides \(x\text{,}\) so \(y=2z\) and \(x=5w\) for some integers \(z\) and \(w\text{.}\) Inserting these into the original equation, we get a new equation

\begin{equation*} 10w = 2(5w)=5(2z) = 10z\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus, \(w=z\text{.}\) Hence, any solution \((x,y)\) must be of the form \((x,y)=(5w,2w)\) for some integer \(w\text{.}\) We can also see that each pair of integers of the form \((5w,2w)\) is a solution. Thus, the solutions to \(2x=5y\) are exactly all pairs of integers \(\{(5w,2w) : w\in\mathbb{Z}\}\text{.}\)
