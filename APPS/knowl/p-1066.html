Recall that a natural number \(n\) is perfect if it is the sum of all its proper divisors (that is, all positive divisors other than \(n\)). For example, \(1 + 2 + 3 = 6\text{,}\) so \(6\) is perfect. If \(p\) is a prime and \(n\in\mathbb{N}\text{,}\) when is \(p^{n}\) perfect?
