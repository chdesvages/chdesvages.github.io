Let \(a, b \in \Z\text{.}\) If there is a linear combination of \(a\) and \(b\) that gives \(1\text{,}\) then \(a\) and \(b\) are coprime. Indeed, if \(m,n \in \Z\) have the property that \(ma+nb = 1\text{,}\) then any common divisor \(d\) of \(a\) and \(b\) must also divide \(1\text{.}\) But then \(d=1\text{,}\) so \(\gcd(a,b)=1\text{.}\)
