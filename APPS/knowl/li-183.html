Item.

(Uniqueness) The factorization is unique: If we also have

\begin{equation*} p_1^{r_{1}}\cdots p_k^{r_{k}} = n = q_1^{s_1}\cdots q_\ell^{s_{\ell}} \end{equation*}

where \(q_{1}\lt \cdots \lt q_{\ell}\) are primes and \(s_{i}> 0\text{,}\) then \(k=\ell\text{,}\) \(p_i=q_i\text{,}\) and \(r_{i}=s_{i}\) for all \(i\text{.}\)
