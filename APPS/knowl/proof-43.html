Proof

Suppose \(\sqrt{n}\in\mathbb{Q}\text{.}\) Thus \(\sqrt{n} = \frac{a}{b}\text{,}\) and \(\frac{a^2}{b^2} = n\text{.}\) By Lemma 8.6.4, we may assume that \(a\) and \(b\) are coprime and positive. Now since \(n = \frac{n}{1}\text{,}\) the uniqueness of Lemma 8.6.4 implies that \(b^2=1\text{.}\) Since \(b\) is positive, it follows that \(b = 1\text{.}\)
