Let \(f=\left(\begin{matrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \amp 5 \amp 6\cr1 \amp 5 \amp 3 \amp 2 \amp 4 \amp 6 \cr \end{matrix} \right)\in\mathrm{S}_6\text{.}\) If we are given that \(f\) is a cycle, then we can figure out its cycle notation by first finding the values \(a_{1},...,a_{r}\) that \(f\) cycles through, that is, the values \(k\) for which \(f(k)\neq k\text{.}\) These are \(2,5\text{,}\) and \(4\text{,}\) and \(f\) leaves all other elements of \(\{1,2,3,4,5,6\}\) fixed (that is \(f(k)=k\) for \(k\not\in \{2,5,4\}\)). Then we just look at where \(f\) sends these numbers in succession:

\begin{equation*} f\big(2\big)=5,\ f\big(5\big)=4,\ f\big(4\big)=2\text{,} \end{equation*}

Thus, \(f=(2\ 5\ 4)\text{.}\)
