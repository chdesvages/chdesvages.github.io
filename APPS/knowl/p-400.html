There exists a complete ordered field. Furthermore, it is unique in the following sense: if \(F\) and \(F'\) are complete ordered fields, then there exists a unique bijection \(\phi \colon F \to F'\) such that

  1. \(\phi(0)=0\text{.}\)

  2. \(\phi(1)=1\text{.}\)

  3. For every \(x,y\in F\text{,}\) one has \(\phi(x+y)=\phi(x)+\phi(y)\text{.}\)

  4. For every \(x,y\in F\text{,}\) one has \(\phi(x\times y)=\phi(x)\times\phi(y)\text{.}\)

  5. For every \(x,y\in F\text{,}\) if \(x\lt y\text{,}\) then \(\phi(x)\lt \phi(y)\text{.}\)
