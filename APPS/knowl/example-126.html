Example 16.7.2.

Let \(f=\left(\begin{matrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \amp 5 \amp 6\cr4 \amp 6 \amp 1 \amp 5 \amp 3 \amp 2 \cr \end{matrix} \right)\in\mathrm{S}_{6}\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} f(1)=4,\ f(4)=5,\ f(5)=3,\ f(3)=1\text{,} \end{equation*}

so one of the cycles in the cycle decomposition of \(f\) is \((1\; 4 \; 5 \; 3)\text{.}\) Now we look at the cylce that contains \(2\) (where we picked \(2\) because it is not one of the integers we have placed in a cycle so far), we see

\begin{equation*} f(2)=6,\ f(6)=2\text{,} \end{equation*}

which implies that \(f=(1\ 4\ 5\ 3)(2\ 6)\text{.}\) Recall that disjoint cycles commute, so we could also write \(f\) as \(=(2\ 6)(1\ 4\ 5\ 3)\text{.}\)
