Let \(f\) be any permutation in \(\mathrm{S}_n\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} 1\mapsto f(1),\ 2\mapsto f(2),\ 3\mapsto f(3), \ldots,\ n\mapsto f(n)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where all numbers \(f(1), f(2), f(3),\ldots, f(n)\) are different (here \(x\mapsto y\) means that \(f(x)=y\text{,}\) or that “\(x\) is mapped to \(y\) under \(f\)”.
