Let's return to the example of counting how many natural numbers at most 100 are divisible by \(5\) or \(3\text{.}\) Let \(A\) be those numbers divisible by \(3\) and \(B\) those numbers which are divisible by \(5\text{.}\) Then as we showed before, \(|A|=33\) and \(|B|=20\text{.}\) Note that the set we want to count is \(A\cup B\text{,}\) which are those numbers either in \(A\) or in \(B\) (that is, divisible by \(3\) or divisible by \(5\)). Also note that \(A\cap B\) are those numbers divisible by both \(3\) and \(5\text{,}\) that is, divisible by 15, of which there are only \(6\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{equation*} |A\cup B|=|A|+|B|-|A\cap B|=33+20-6 = 47\text{.} \end{equation*}
