Let \(n\in\mathbb{N}\) and let \(f\in S_{n}\text{.}\) Then \(f\) can be written as a product of cycles of lengths \(r_{1},....,r_{k}\) where \(r_{i}\in \mathbb{N}\) and \(r_{1}+\cdots + r_{k}=n\text{.}\) Then the order is the least common multiple of these numbers, which is at most

\begin{equation*} r_{1}\cdots r_{k}\lt 2^{r_{1}}\cdots 2^{r_{k}}=2^{r_{1}+\cdots + r_{k}}=2^{n}\text{.} \end{equation*}
