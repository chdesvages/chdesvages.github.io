Lemma 8.2.5. Linear Combination Lemma.

Let \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(d\) be integers. If \(d\) divides both \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) then \(d\) divides every integer linear combination of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) In other words, for every pair of integers \(m,n\in\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) the integer \(d\) also divides \(ma + nb\text{.}\)
