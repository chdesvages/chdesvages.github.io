Solution 11.5.1.2.

Let \(y=x^2\text{.}\) Then \(y^2+y+1=0\text{,}\) and the solutions to this are

\begin{equation*} x^2=y=\frac{1\pm \sqrt{5}}{2} \end{equation*}

Thus, we see that \(x=\pm \sqrt{\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}}\) are two solutions, we just need to find the other two. They will be solutions to \(x^2 = \frac{1-\sqrt{5}}{2} = - \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}\text{,}\) which are \(\pm i \sqrt{\frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}}\text{.}\) Thus, all 4 solutions are

\begin{equation*} \pm \sqrt{\frac{1+\sqrt{5}}{2}}, \;\; \pm i \sqrt{\frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
