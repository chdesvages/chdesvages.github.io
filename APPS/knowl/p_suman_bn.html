Proposition 9.2.4.

Let \((a_{j})\) and \((b_{j})\) be sequences. If \(\sum_{j=1}^\infty a_{j}\) and \(\sum_{j=1}^\infty b_{j}\) are convergent, then so is \(\sum_{j=1}^\infty (a_{j}+b_{j})\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} (a_{j}+b_{j})=\sum_{j=1}^{\infty} a_{j}+\sum_{j=1}^{\infty} b_{j}\text{.} \end{equation*}

If \(c\in\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) then \(\sum ca_{j}\) is convergent and

\begin{equation*} \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} ca_{j}=c \sum_{j=1}^{\infty} a_{j}\text{.} \end{equation*}
