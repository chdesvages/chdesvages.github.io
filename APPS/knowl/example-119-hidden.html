Let

\begin{equation*} f= \left(\begin{matrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \cr2 \amp 3 \amp 1 \amp 4 \cr \end{matrix} \right), \;\;\; \mbox{ and } \;\;\; g= \left(\begin{matrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \cr4 \amp 3 \amp 2 \amp 1 \cr \end{matrix} \right) \end{equation*}

We can determine \(fg\) by plugging in each number \(k\in \{1,2,3,4\}\) and see what \(g(k)\) and then \(f(g(k))\) is:

\begin{equation*} \left(\begin{array}{c|cccc} k \amp 1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \\ g(k) \amp 4 \amp 3 \amp 2 \amp 1 \crf(g(k)) \amp 4 \amp 1 \amp 3 \amp 2 \end{array} \right) \end{equation*}

Thus, taking the top and bottom rows, we see that

\begin{equation*} fg = \left(\begin{matrix}1 \amp 2 \amp 3 \amp 4 \cr4 \amp 1 \amp 3 \amp 2 \cr \end{matrix} \right) \end{equation*}
