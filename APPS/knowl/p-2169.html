We would like to define a permutation to be even if it is a product of even number of cycles of order \(2\text{,}\) and odd if it is a product of odd number of cycles of order \(2\text{.}\) However, it is not clear from this definition whether or not a permutation can be both even and odd at the same time. Hence, we will actually define the properties of being even and odd slightly differently, and then later deduce that even and odd permutations can be written as an even or odd number of \(2\)-cycles.
