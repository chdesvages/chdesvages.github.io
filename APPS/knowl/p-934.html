In \(\mathbb{Z}/{5}\text{,}\) does \(2\) have a cubed root? Let us test the values:

  • \(0^{3}=0\text{.}\)

  • \(1^{3}=1\text{.}\)

  • \(2^{3}=3\) since \(2^{3} = 8 = 3\mod 5\text{.}\)

  • \(3^{3}=2\) since \(3^{3} = 27 = 2\mod 5\text{.}\)

  • \(4^{3}=4\) since \(4^{3} = (-1)^{3}=-1 = 4 \mod 5\text{.}\)
