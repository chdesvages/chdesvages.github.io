Proposition 5.2.3.

Let \((a_{n})\) and \((b_{n})\) be sequences of real numbers that converge to \(a\) and \(b\) respectively. Then

  1. \(\displaystyle a_{n}+b_{n}\rightarrow a+b\)

  2. \(\displaystyle a_{n}b_{n} \rightarrow ab\)

  3. If \(c\in\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) then \(ca_{n}\rightarrow ca\text{.}\)

  4. If \(b\neq 0\) and \(b_n\neq 0\) for all \(n\text{,}\) then \(\frac{a_{n}}{b_{n}}\rightarrow \frac{a}{b}\text{.}\)
