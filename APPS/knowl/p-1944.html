However, this is not the final answer. Notice that in this algorithm, each diagonal actually gets counted twice, since for two nonadjacent corners \(v\) and \(w\text{,}\) \(\overline{vw}=\overline{wv}\text{.}\) What we have really counted is the number of ordered pairs \((v,w)\) of corners that are not adjacent, and every diagonal corresponds to exactly two such pairs. Thus, if we divide \(n(n-3)\) by two, we get the true number of diagonals, that is, \(n(n-3)/2\text{.}\) (And note that either \(n\) or \(n-3\) is even!)
