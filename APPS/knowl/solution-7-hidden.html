One could have A= “\(n\) is a prime bigger than 2” and B= “\(n\) is odd.”

Recall that \(A\Rightarrow B\) is equivalent to “\(\bar{A}\) or \(B\text{,}\)” and \(B\Rightarrow A\) is equivalent to “\(\bar{B}\) or \(A\text{,}\)” which have truth tables

\(A\) \(B\) \(\bar{A}\) \(\bar{A}\) or \({B}\)
T T F T
T F F F
F T T T
F F T T
\(A\) \(B\) \(\bar{B}\) \(\bar{B}\) or \({A}\)
T T F T
T F F T
F T T T
F F T F

Since the truth tables are not the same, they are not equivalent.
