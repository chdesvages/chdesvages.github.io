We now aim to show that such a real number \(y\) exists. Consider the set

\begin{equation*} S = \{z\in \RR : (z>0)\wedge(z^n\geq x)\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The set \(S\) is nonempty 1 , and by definition, \(0\) is a lower bound for \(S\text{.}\) Hence we may define \(y=\inf S\text{,}\) which is nonnegative. We now aim to prove that \(y^n=x\text{.}\)
