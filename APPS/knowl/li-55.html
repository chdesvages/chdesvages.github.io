We first claim that \(\gcd(a,b)\) divides \(r_{n}\text{.}\) Note that \(r_0=b\) and \(r_1=a\) are certainly linear combinations of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) Let \(k\in \N\) with \(1\leq k \leq n\text{.}\) If \(r_{k-1}\) and \(r_k\) are each integer linear combinations of \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) so is \(r_{k+1}\text{.}\) By induction, we conclude that \(r_n\) is a integer linear combination of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) Hence it follows from the Linear Combination LemmaÂ that \(\gcd(a,b)\) divides \(r_n\text{.}\)
