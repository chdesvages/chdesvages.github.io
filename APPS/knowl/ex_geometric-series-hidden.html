The quintessential example of a convergent series is the geometric series. Recall that for any real number \(r \neq 1\text{,}\)

\begin{equation} \sum_{j=0}^{n} ar ^{j}=\frac{a(1-r^{n+1})}{1-r}\tag{9.2.1} \end{equation}

If \(|r|\lt 1\text{,}\) then \(|r^{n+1}|=|r|^{n+1}\rightarrow 0\) by ExampleÂ 8.1.4, and so \(r^{n+1}\rightarrow 0\) as well. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \limn \sum_{j=0}^{n}ar^{j} = \limn \frac{a(1-r^{n+1})}{1-r} = \frac{\limn a(1-r^{n+1})}{1-r}=\frac{a- a\limn r^{n+1}}{1-r}=\frac{a}{1-r}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus, when \(|r|\lt 1\text{,}\) \(\sum_{j=0}^\infty a r^{j}\) is convergent, and \(\sum_{j=0}^{\infty} a r^{j}=\frac{a}{1-r}\text{.}\) We'll see below that this series converges if and only if \(|r|\lt 1\text{.}\)
