For part b, we can prove this by strong induction on \(|z|^2\text{.}\) If \(|z|^2=1\text{,}\) then \(z\) must be \(\pm 1\text{,}\) which is a primo (since if we have \(xy=z\text{,}\) then \(1=|z|=|xy|=|x||y|\text{,}\) so \(|x|=|y|=1\text{,}\) and hence \(x,y=\pm 1\text{,}\) that is, they are both equal to plus or minus z).
