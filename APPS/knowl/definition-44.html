Definition 8.2.1.

Let \(a\) and \(b\) be two nonzero integers. A common divisor or common factor of \(a\) and \(b\) is an integer \(d \in \N\) such that \(d\) divides both \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) The greatest common divisor (AKA the highest common factor) of \(a\) and \(b\) is the largest common divisor of \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\) We denote this integer \(d\) by \(\gcd(a,b)\) (you may also see the notation \(\mathrm{hcf}(a,b)\text{.}\)

If \(\gcd(a,b)=1\text{,}\) then we say that \(a\) and \(b\) are said to be coprime or relatively prime.
