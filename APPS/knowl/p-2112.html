Let \(\{a_{1},a_{2},\ldots,a_{r}\}\) be a non-empty subset of the set \(\{1,2,\ldots,n\}\text{.}\) The permutation \(f\in\mathrm{S}_{n}\) such that

\begin{equation*} f\big(a_{1}\big)=a_{2},\ f\big(a_{2}\big)=a_{3}, \ldots,\ f\big(a_{r-1}\big)=a_{r},\ f\big(a_{r}\big)=a_{1} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} f(k)=k\iff k\not\in\{a_{1},a_{2},\ldots,a_{r}\} \end{equation*}

is denoted by

\begin{equation*} \big(a_{1}\ a_{2}\ \cdots\ a_{r}\big) \end{equation*}

and is called a cycle or cyclic permutation (of length \(r\)).
