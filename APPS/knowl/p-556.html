Let \(L=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_{n}\text{.}\) By the definition of a limit (with \(\epsilon=1\)), there is a natural number \(N\) such that if \(n> N\text{,}\) then \(|a_{n}-L|\lt 1\text{.}\)  1  Thus, for \(n> N\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} |a_{n}|=|a_{n}-L+L| \leq |a_{n}-L|+|L| \lt 1+|L|\text{.} \end{equation*}
