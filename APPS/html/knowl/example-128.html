Example 16.7.4.

  • The permutation \(f=(1\ 4\ 5\ 3)(2\ 6)(7)\in S_{7}\) has cycle shape \((4,2,1)\text{.}\)

  • The permutation \(g=(1 \; 2) (3 \; 4) \in S_{4}\) has cycle shape \((2,2)\text{,}\) or more succinctly, \((2^2)\text{.}\)

  • The permutation \(h = (1 \; 4)(2\; 3) (\;5 \; 7)(8 \; 9 \;11)\) in \(S_{11}\) has cycle shape \((3,2^3,1^2)\text{.}\) Why the \(1^2\text{?}\) Recall that \(6\) and \(10\) don't appear in any of the cycles in \(h\text{,}\) so \(h(6)=6\) and \(h(10)=10\text{,}\) so really the full cycle decomposition of \(h\) is \((1 \; 4)(2\; 3) (\;5 \; 7)(8 \; 9 \;11)(6)(10)\text{.}\)
