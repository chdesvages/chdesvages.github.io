In the case of our second example, the two ordered partitions \((\{1,2\}, \{3,4\}, \{5,6\})\) and \((\{3,4\}, \{1,2\}, \{5,6\})\) are distinct because in the first case \(1\) and \(2\) are in the red bin while in the second case they are in the blue bin; we want to count these separately. In general we think of having \(k\) colours, and the set \(A_1\) as being assigned colour \(1\text{,}\) \(A_2\) as being assigned colour \(2\text{,}\) and so on.
