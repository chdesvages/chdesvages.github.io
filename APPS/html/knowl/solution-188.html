Solution 16.10.1.1.
\begin{equation*} f=(1 \; 3 \; 4 \; 8 )(2 \; 5 \;7 ) ,\;\; f^2 = (1 \; 4)(2 \; 7 \; 5)(3 \; 8 ),\;\; f^{-1} = (8 \; 4 \; 3 \; 1)(7 \; 5 \; 2) \end{equation*}

To compute \(f^{146}\text{,}\) note thatthe order of \(f\) is \(lcm(4,3)=12\text{,}\) and \(146 = 12\cdot 12 + 2\text{,}\) thus

\begin{equation*} f^{146} = (f^{12})^{12}\cdot f^2 = f^2 = (1 \; 4)(2 \; 7 \; 5)(3 \; 8 )\text{.} \end{equation*}
