Definition 10.1.1. Complex Numbers.

Define \(i\) to be a number such that \(i^2=-1\text{.}\)

The complex numbers are any number of the form

\begin{equation*} z=x+iy\mbox{ where } x,y \in \mathbb{R}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The set of all complex numbers is denoted \(\mathbb{C}\text{.}\)

We define \(\mathrm{Re}(z)=x\) and \(\mathrm{Im}(z)=y\) to be the real and imaginary parts of \(z\text{.}\)
