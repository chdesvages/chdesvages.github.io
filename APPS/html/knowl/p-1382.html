We note that this is easily completely factorized over the complex numbers. Let \(\xi = e^{\frac{2\pi i}{10}}\text{,}\) then the \(5\)th roots of unity are \(1,\xi,\xi^2, \xi^3,\xi^4\text{.}\) By examining these numbers, we can see that \(\xi^{4}=\overline{\xi}\) and \(\xi^{3} = \overline{\xi}^{2}\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{equation*} x^5+1 = (x+1)\underbrace{(x-\xi)(x-\bar{\xi})}_{\textrm{conjugate} }\underbrace{(x-\xi^2)(x-\bar{\xi}^2)}_{\textrm{conjugate} }\text{.} \end{equation*}
