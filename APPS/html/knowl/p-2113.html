We call these “cycles” since, if you imagine arranging the numbers \(a_{1},...,a_{r}\) around a circle, \(f\) just rotates these numbers. In other words, this is the permutation that sends \(a_{1}\) to \(a_{2}\text{,}\) \(a_{2}\) to \(a_{3}\text{,...,}\) and \(a_{r}\) to \(a_{1}\text{,}\) and leaves everything else alone.
