To find the roots of \(x^4+1\text{,}\) we need to find solutions to \(x^4=-1=e^{\pi i}\text{.}\) One solution is \(z=e^{\pi i/4}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}i\text{.}\) To find all 4 roots, we just multiply this by the 4th roots of unity, which are \(\pm 1, \pm 1\text{,}\) thus all solutions which will be

\begin{equation*} \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}i \end{equation*}

where we consider all 4 possible combinations of \(+\)'s and \(-\)'s. Let \(z=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}+i\frac{1}{sqrt{2}}\) and \(w=-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}+i\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\text{.}\) Then the roots are \(z,w,\overline{z},\overline{w}\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{align*} x^4+1 \amp = (x-z)(z-\overline{z})(x-w)(x-\overline{w})\\ \amp =(x^2 -zx-\overline{z}x+z\overline{z})(x^2 -wx-\overline{w}x+w\overline{w})\\ \amp = (x^2-\sqrt{2}x+|z|^2)(x^2+\sqrt{2}x+|w|^2)\\ \amp = (x^2-\sqrt{2}x+1)(x^2+\sqrt{2}x+1)\text{.} \end{align*}
