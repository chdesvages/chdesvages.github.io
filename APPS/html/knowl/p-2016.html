Let \(n\geq 2\) and suppose \(A_{1},A_{2}, \dots ,A_{n}\) are finite sets. Let

\begin{equation*} N_{1} = |A_{1}|+|A_{2}|+\cdots +|A_n|\text{,} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} N_{2} = |A_{1}\cap A_{2}|+|A_{1}\cap A_{3}|+|A_{2}\cap A_{3}|+\cdots +|A_{n-1} \cap A_n|\text{,} \end{equation*}

and similarly, let

\begin{equation*} N_k = \sum_{1 \leq i_1 \lt i_2 \lt \dots \lt i_k \leq n} |A_{i_1} \cap A_{i_2} \cap \dots \cap A_{i_k}| \end{equation*}

be the sum of the sizes of the intersections of all collections of \(k\) different \(A_i\)'s, so that

\begin{equation*} N_{n} = |\bigcap_{k=1}^{n}A_{k}|\text{.} \end{equation*}
