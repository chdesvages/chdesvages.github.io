Proof

Let \(f=(a_{1}\; a_{2}\; \cdots \; a_{r}\}\) and \(g=(b_{1}\; b_{2}\; \cdots \; b_{s}\}\) where \(\{a_{1},...,a_{r}\}\) and \(\{b_{1},...,b_{s}\}\) are disjoint sets in \(\{1,2,...,n\}\text{.}\) Let us look at the charts for \(f\) and \(f\circ g\text{,}\) but ordering the first row so that they begin with \(a_{1},...,a_{r},b_{1},...,b_{s}\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \left(\begin{array}{c|ccccccccccc} k \amp a_1 \amp a_2 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r-1} \amp a_r \amp b_{1} \amp b_{2} \amp ... \amp b_{s-1} \amp b_{s} \amp ... \\ g(k) \amp a_1 \amp a_2 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r-1} \amp a_r \amp b_{2} \amp b_{3} \amp ... \amp b_{s} \amp b_{1} \amp ... \\ f(g(k)) \amp a_2 \amp a_3 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r} \amp a_1 \amp b_{2} \amp b_{3} \amp ... \amp b_{s} \amp b_{1} \amp ... \end{array} \right) \end{equation*}

Above, the final numbers in “\(..\text{.}\)” are the remaining integers \(\{1,2,...,n\}\) not in either \(\{a_{1},...,a_{r}\}\) or \(\{b_{1},...,b_{s}\}\text{,}\) and for \(k\) in this range, we have \(f(k)=g(k)=k\text{.}\) Notice that since the \(a\)'s are not in \(\{b_{1},...,b_{s}\}\text{,}\) \(g\) fixes the numbers \(a_{1},...,a_{r}\) (that is, \(g(a_{i})=a_{i}\) for \(i=1,...,r\)) and then cycles through the \(b\)'s. Similarly, since the \(b\)'s are not in \(\{a_{1},...,a_{r}\}\text{,}\) \(f\) fixes the numbers \(b_{1},...,b_{s}\) (that is, \(f(b_{i})=b_{i}\) for \(i=1,...,s\)) and then cycles through the \(a\)'s. Thus, we get that

\begin{equation*} fg= \left(\begin{array}{c|cccccccccc} a_1 \amp a_2 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r-1} \amp a_r \amp b_{1} \amp b_{2} \amp ... \amp b_{r-1} \amp b_{r} \amp ... \\ a_2 \amp a_3 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r} \amp a_1 \amp b_{2} \amp b_{3} \amp ... \amp b_{r} \amp b_{1} \amp ... \end{array} \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Notice that if we did the exact same computation but with \(gf\) instead of \(fg\text{,}\) we would get the same chart, thus \(fg=gf\text{.}\)
