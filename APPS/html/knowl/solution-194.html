Solution 1.

Note that since \((1 \; 2 \; \cdots \; k-1 )\in T\text{,}\) \(T\) generates \((1 \; 2 \; \cdots \; k-1 )^{-1}\text{,}\) and so it also generates

\begin{equation*} (1 \; 2 \; \cdots \; k-1 )^{-1} (1 \; 2 \; \cdots \; k-1 \; k) =(k-1 \; k) \end{equation*}

for every \(k\text{.}\) We have already shown that every \(f\) is a product of elements in \(S_{n}\) is a product of permutations of the form \((k-1\; k)\text{,}\) and now we have shown that each such permutation is a product of elements in \(T\text{,}\) thus \(T\) generates \(S_n\text{.}\)
