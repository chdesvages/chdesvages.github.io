We say a set \(S\subseteq S_n\) generates \(f\in S_{n}\) there are \(f_{1},...,f_{k}\in S\) so that \(f=f_{1}\cdots f_{k}\) (where we could have \(k=1\text{,}\) that is, this could just be a composition of just one function), and \(S\) generates \(S_{n}\) if it generates \(f\) for every \(f\in S_{n}\text{.}\)

  1. Show that if \(f\in S\text{,}\) then \(S\) generates \(\iota\) and \(f^{-1}\text{.}\) Solution.

  2. Show that \(S=\{ (1\; 2), (2 \; 3),...,(n-1 \; n)\}\) generates \(S_{n}\text{.}\) Solution.

  3. Show that \(T=\{(1\; 2),(1\; 2 \; 3),..., (1 \; 2 \; ...\; n)\}\) generates \(S_{n}\text{.}\) Solution.
