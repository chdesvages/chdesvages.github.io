Assume \(P\Rightarrow Q\text{,}\) \(Q\Rightarrow R\) and \(R\Rightarrow P\text{,}\) we will show that \(P\Leftrightarrow R\text{.}\) To prove this, we need to show \(P\Rightarrow R\) and \(R\Rightarrow P\text{.}\) We area already assuming \(R\Rightarrow P\text{,}\) so we just need to prove \(P\Rightarrow R\text{.}\) Hence, assume \(P\text{.}\) Then since \(P\Rightarrow Q\text{,}\) we know \(Q\) is also true, and since \(Q\Rightarrow R\text{,}\) we also know \(R\) is true. Thus \(P\Rightarrow R\text{.}\)
