Proof

These each follow by direct computation. Let us denote \(u=a+bi\text{,}\) \(v=c+di\text{,}\) for \(a,b,c,d\in\mathbb{R}\text{.}\)

  • We have:

    \begin{equation*} \overline{u+v} = \overline{(a+c) + (b+d)i} = a+c - (b+d)i = (a-bi) + (c-di) = \overline{u} + \overline{v}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  • We have:

    \begin{align*} \overline{u\cdot v} \amp = \overline{(a+bi)(c+di)}\\ \amp = \overline{(ac-bd) + (bc+ad)i}\\ \amp = ac-bd - (bc+ad)i\\ \amp = (a-bi)(c-di)\\ \amp = \overline{u}\cdot\overline{v}\text{.} \end{align*}

  • We have:

    \begin{equation*} |uv|^2 = (uv)(\overline{u}\overline{v}) = u\overline{u}v\overline{v} = |u|^2\cdot|v|^2\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where we have used (b) and (c) above. Since both sides of the final equality are non-negatives, the inequality holds if, and only if, it holds after taking square roots (as we showed in Chapter 5, on inequalities)

Hence, we have established each of the four equalities.
