Above, the final numbers in “\(..\text{.}\)” are the remaining integers \(\{1,2,...,n\}\) not in either \(\{a_{1},...,a_{r}\}\) or \(\{b_{1},...,b_{s}\}\text{,}\) and for \(k\) in this range, we have \(f(k)=g(k)=k\text{.}\) Notice that since the \(a\)'s are not in \(\{b_{1},...,b_{s}\}\text{,}\) \(g\) fixes the numbers \(a_{1},...,a_{r}\) (that is, \(g(a_{i})=a_{i}\) for \(i=1,...,r\)) and then cycles through the \(b\)'s. Similarly, since the \(b\)'s are not in \(\{a_{1},...,a_{r}\}\text{,}\) \(f\) fixes the numbers \(b_{1},...,b_{s}\) (that is, \(f(b_{i})=b_{i}\) for \(i=1,...,s\)) and then cycles through the \(a\)'s. Thus, we get that

\begin{equation*} fg= \left(\begin{array}{c|cccccccccc} a_1 \amp a_2 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r-1} \amp a_r \amp b_{1} \amp b_{2} \amp ... \amp b_{r-1} \amp b_{r} \amp ... \\ a_2 \amp a_3 \amp \cdots \amp a_{r} \amp a_1 \amp b_{2} \amp b_{3} \amp ... \amp b_{r} \amp b_{1} \amp ... \end{array} \right)\text{.} \end{equation*}
